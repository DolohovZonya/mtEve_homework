According to the phylogenetic tree distances, on average we have around 40 mutations between african branch and other branches. The mutation rate was taken from this paper: https://www.nature.com/articles/35047064, which estimates this value to be 1.7*10^-8 mutations per site per year. Thus, the mt Eve age can be estimated as 40 / (1.7*10^-8*16569), where 16569 is the mtGenome size. From this, mt Eve age is near 142,000 years (other halpogroups separated from africans around 142,000 years ago).
